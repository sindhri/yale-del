The summer internship for undergraduates is designed for college students who are either currently studying or are interested in psychology/psychiatry or child-related medicine.

NATURE OF INTERNSHIP: Interns will join a clinical/investigative group working primarily with a high-risk population of children ranging in age from three months to fourteen years. Interns will have the opportunity to interact with leading child psychiatrists and neuroscientists who are studying early brain development, working with children with pervasive developmental/psychiatric disorders. This experience is structured to provide a view of both medical research and clinical medical care as practiced in an academic department of a medical school.

SKILLS AND KNOWLEDGE INTERNS WILL GAIN: Interns will gain a basic introduction to developmental pediatrics and child development. The internship will help develop skills in administering developmental computer tasks to children and in reviewing medical records. Interns will also gain an introduction to semi-structured and open-ended interviewing techniques for adults and children.

INTERN'S DUTIES: Interns will assist in the developmental and psychological evaluations of
children; complete behavioral scoring protocols from videotaped records of assessments; and review, under supervision, medical records of participating children. Opportunities to participate in other lab projects will also arise.

COMPENSATION: Interns will be compensated for their time and work with $2,500.00 for the ten week duration of the internship.
