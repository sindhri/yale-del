Kathryn Armstrong

Kathryn is one of the research coordinators for the Laboratory for Cognitive-Emotional Development. She has been involved with the laboratory's ongoing research for more than eleven years. She has a B.A. from the College of Wooster and a M.A. from Virginia Tech.
 
Nicki Hunter

Nicki is a research assistant and currently the coordinator of the Startle lab and the Genetics Project. She is also a tester for both IFU and YCLS. Nicki is originally from Texas.
 
Jessica Bratt

Jessica is a research assistant for IFU and YCLS. She attended Calvin College in her hometown of Grand Rapids, Michigan, earning her BA in both psychology and theology, and graduated with honors in psychology. She also attended Princeton Theological Seminary from which she received her Master of Divinity degree. Before coming to the Child Study Center, Jessica was a hospital chaplain at Yale-New Haven Hospital, where she completed the Clinical Pastoral Education residency program.
 
Dania Braumstein

Dania has a Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology and is a Clinical Instructor at the Child Study Center. She has recently joined both the IFU and YCLS projects. Dania has also been working with Dr. Mayes on revising a therapy manual geared toward increasing mentalization capacities in families with young children.
 
Eric Langlois

Eric has worked in the lab as a research assistant for over two years on various projects including the Infants and Children Follow-up Project and a study in conjunction with the Department of Surgery assessing the neurocognitive changes in hyperparathyroidism. He received his B.A. in Psychology from SCSU where he graduated Magna Cum Laude with departmental honors and currently serves as the coordinator of the high-density electroencephalography laboratory.
