Young Children's Learning Study

This study explores the impact of poverty on learning and cognition in children, specifically, the ways in which growing up in poverty might impact the development of neurocognitive executive control functions such as inhibition and working memory. In particular, we focus on inhibitory executive control functions that include inhibition in attention and inhibition in action. These kinds of executive control capacities are particularly central to learning and academic performance. Studying these inhibitory processes within executive control functions is particularly pertinent to studies of economically disadvantaged populations in that a number of developmental delays and/or developmental disorders involving dysregulated inhibition are said to be common.
